FINER Evaluation

SMART refines how your question is worded. FINER evaluates whether the research project behind that question is worthy of pursuit. Complete each dimension to build your case for the proposal.

F — Feasible

Pending

Can the project be completed with available data, time, and access?

I — Interesting

Pending

Would researchers, practitioners, and your committee find this study compelling enough to pursue?

N — Novel

Pending

Does the study add something new to knowledge not just confirm what is already known?

E — Ethical

Pending

Can the study be conducted ethically, with informed consent and without undue risk to participants?

R — Relevant

Pending

Will the findings inform meaningful organizational decisions, policy, or managerial practice?

Refinement Summary & Next Steps

Your Research Question

Complete the steps to see your finalized question.

    Strong Example (Quantitative)

    To what extent does quarterly leadership coaching reduce voluntary turnover among customer service teams in a Midwest healthcare system over the next 12 months?

    Strong Example (Qualitative)

    What are the lived experiences of mid-level managers regarding psychological safety during post-merger organizational change, and how do leadership behaviors shape those experiences?

    Weak Example

    How can we improve leadership in our company?

    Missing: specificity, measurable variables, timeframe, population, and scholarly context.

    Common Pitfalls & Fixes

    • Too broad? Narrow to a manageable unit a team, department, or defined timeframe.
    • Unclear variables? Revisit Step 4 to sharpen independent, dependent, and control factors.
    • No gap? Revisit Step 3 and connect your study explicitly to the scholarly literature.
    • Ethical flags? Consult DBA IRB guidance and mock application templates.
    • FINER "Interesting" weak? Be specific about the practitioner problem and field stakes.
